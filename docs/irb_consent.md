## Purpose of the Study

You are invited to participate in a research study examining how people perceive AI-generated responses to individuals seeking emotional support. The purpose is to understand how different AI system designs affect the perceived quality, safety, and trustworthiness of supportive responses.

## What You Will Be Asked to Do

If you agree to participate, you will:

1. Read **12 short scenarios** depicting fictional individuals seeking emotional support. Topics range from everyday stress to more serious situations (including suicidal thoughts, self-harm, and domestic violence).

3. Complete a brief **post-study questionnaire** including demographics and open-ended feedback.

# Debriefing Form

**Study Title**: Evaluating AI-Generated Supportive Responses for Mental Health Scenarios

## What Was This Study About?

Thank you for participating! This study investigated how different AI system architectures affect people's perceptions of emotionally supportive responses.

You evaluated responses generated under one of three conditions (without knowing which):

| Condition | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Single Agent** | A single AI generates an empathetic response with no safety review. |
| **Hidden Checker** | A second AI reviews the response for safety concerns, but you see only the final, clean response. |
| **Visible Checker** | A second AI reviews for safety, and you see a "✓ Safety reviewed" indicator alongside the response. |

The scenarios were **fictional** and designed to test how AI systems handle a range of emotional situations — from everyday stress to high-risk situations like suicidal ideation. No real people were involved in these scenarios.

## Why Does This Matter?

AI chatbots are increasingly used for emotional support, but they can sometimes give harmful advice (e.g., validating self-harm or failing to recommend professional help). Our research explores whether a "maker-checker" architecture — where one AI generates and another reviews — can improve safety without sacrificing empathy.

## Your Contribution

Your ratings will help us understand whether visible safety checking affects perceived trust, warmth, and willingness to seek professional help. This information will guide the design of safer AI support systems.
