# Informed Consent Form

## Study Title

**Evaluating AI-Assisted Mental-Health Support: A Comparison of System Designs**

## Researchers

[Your name], [Department], [University]

## Purpose

We are studying how different designs of AI-based emotional support systems
affect user experience. You will interact with an AI system that provides
supportive responses to hypothetical emotional scenarios, and then answer a
survey about your experience.

## What You Will Do

1. Read a brief introduction to the system.
2. Interact with the AI system across 3–5 pre-defined scenarios (you will
   read the scenario and view the system's response).
3. Complete a post-interaction questionnaire (approximately 10 minutes).
4. Optionally answer a few open-ended questions.

**Estimated total time: 20–30 minutes.**

## Important Information

- **This is not real therapy.** The AI system you interact with is a research
  prototype. It is not a licensed counsellor, therapist, or medical
  professional.
- You will be reading **hypothetical** scenarios — you are not required to
  share any personal information about your own mental health.
- Some scenarios involve sensitive topics such as anxiety, depression, and
  emotional distress. If any scenario causes discomfort, you may skip it or
  withdraw at any time.
- Your participation is entirely **voluntary**. You may withdraw at any point
  without penalty.

## Data & Privacy

- Your responses will be anonymised and stored securely.
- No personally identifiable information will be collected beyond basic
  demographics (age range, gender, education level).
- Data will be used only for academic research and may be reported in
  aggregate in publications.
- Raw data will not be shared publicly.

## Risks & Safeguards

- **Minimal risk.** You will be reading hypothetical scenarios, not discussing
  your own mental health.
- If you experience any distress during or after the study, please contact:
  - **National Suicide Prevention Lifeline**: 988 (US)
  - **Crisis Text Line**: Text HOME to 741741 (US)
  - **Samaritans**: 116 123 (UK)
  - **IASP Crisis Centres**: https://www.iasp.info/resources/Crisis_Centres/
